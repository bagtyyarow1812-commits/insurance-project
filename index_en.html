History of insurance

The first analogues of insurance are found in Ancient Rome (collegia tenuiorum) and in medieval guilds. In the 17th century marine insurance appeared in London, and in the 19th century – life insurance. In Russia, insurance business began to develop under Catherine II.

Ancient world

  • Babylon (c. 2000 BC) – insurance of trade caravans
  • Roman colleges – mutual aid for burial

Middle Ages

  • Guilds and crafts – help to widows and orphans
  • Lloyd's of London (1688) – marine insurance

Modern era

  • First life insurance society (1762, London)
  • Insurance Society "Russia" (1881)

Market overview

Savings life insurance

Life insurance programs (endowment, unit-linked) allow you to both protect your family and accumulate capital. The difference from a bank deposit is risk coverage and potential investment income.

Protection

In case of death or disability, the insured sum is paid out, which can reach several million rubles.

Savings

Regular contributions form a guaranteed capital by a certain age + additional investment income.

Example: a 20-year policy with an annual premium of 60,000 rubles – by the end of the term the accumulated amount will be about 1.5 million rubles, taking into account the yield.
