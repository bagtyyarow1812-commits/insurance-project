Chapter 3. Modern insurance in Russia:
figures, leaders and technologies

3.1 Structure and quantitative parameters of the insurance market (2000-2020)

By 2020, life insurance had finally established itself as one of the main drivers of growth for the entire Russian insurance market. Despite the COVID-19 pandemic and the economic downturn, it was this segment that demonstrated outstanding results, providing the bulk of the increase in total insurance premiums.

Market volume. At the end of 2020, life insurers collected 541.3 billion rubles in premiums. The market recovered after the slump in the first half of the year, when premiums fell due to the pandemic, and ended the year with positive dynamics. A key role was played by the active promotion of endowment life insurance products through the banking channel, as well as borrower life insurance against the backdrop of a mortgage lending boom.

Leaders of the life insurance market in 2020:

  • SOGAZ-LIFE — made the strongest leap: premiums increased by 66.2% (up to 76.1 billion rubles) due to the acquisition of the VTB Life Insurance portfolio.
  • RSHB-Life Insurance — rose from 13th to 7th place, premium growth of 1.9 times.
  • Sberbank Life Insurance — historical leader, but premiums fell by 30.8% due to market saturation with unit-linked products.
  • AlfaStrakhovanie-Life — strengthened its position, entered the top ten largest insurers in Russia.
  • Renaissance Life — burst into the TOP-10, showing multiple growth.
  • Capital Life — entered the group of growth leaders.
  • Ingosstrakh-Life — maintained a stable position in the top ten.

3.2 Key segments: OSAGO, CASCO, VHI (analysis of actual data)

Motor insurance (OSAGO and CASCO)

Despite a 9.1% drop in new car sales in 2020, the motor insurance market held up due to an increase in the number of contracts and changes in tariffs. Together, CASCO and OSAGO account for 35% of all insurance premiums in Russia, and in the regions this figure reaches 45%.

OSAGO: the leader is RESO-Garantia: in 9 months of 2020, 4.36 million contracts were concluded, premiums 27.33 billion rubles (+19.1%), payments decreased by 8.6%. Other major players: AlfaStrakhovanie, Ingosstrakh, Rosgosstrakh (brand awareness 83%).

CASCO: RESO-Garantia issued 453.6 thousand contracts, premiums 19.85 billion rubles (+6.9%), payments decreased by 0.4%. Overall, CASCO premiums increased by 1.2-3%.

IT technologies in motor insurance: the decrease in payments while premiums grew is a consequence of the introduction of digital systems. Ingosstrakh acquired a Low-Code Platform, VSK launched a Telegram bot. Competition is shifting to the technological plane.

Health insurance (VHI)

2020 was a stress test for VHI: the number of contracts collapsed by 37% (to 10.7 million), but premiums fell only 2% (to 177 billion rubles). The share of individuals fell from 19.9% to 12.3%, the corporate segment grew to 87.7%. The average policy price increased by 55.5% (to 16.5 thousand rubles) due to higher medical costs and the inclusion of COVID-19 testing.

3.3 Leading insurance companies and market shares (2020)

By 2020, an oligopoly had formed on the Russian insurance market. After the VTB Insurance group joined the SOGAZ group, the latter became the largest player with a 26% share of the total market. The top five control more than 50% of the market.

Dynamics of the number of players: the market is actively consolidating. In 2019 — 121 companies in VHI, by the end of 2020 — 104. During 2020, 4 companies left the market due to sanctions, 9 gave up their licenses.

Trust index: old brands have the highest recognition: Rosgosstrakh — 83%, Ingosstrakh — 71%.

Sales channels: banks remain the largest channel — 38% of premiums (34.7 billion rubles). The pandemic strengthened online channels.
